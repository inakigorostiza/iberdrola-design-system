# Iberdrola — Voice, Tone & Imagery

## Brand personality
Warm, optimistic, trustworthy, forward-looking. A 125-year energy company that feels **human and
close**, not corporate-cold. Sustainability and people are at the centre.

## Voice & tone
- **Clear over clever.** Short sentences, plain language, no jargon. ("Tu energía de siempre, con
  tecnología actual.")
- **Warm & direct.** Speak to the reader as *tú*. Encouraging, never pushy.
- **Confident, backed by proof.** Lean on facts and figures (125 años, +11 millones de contratos,
  Nº1 del mercado, 5-star reviews) rather than adjectives.
- **Benefit-first.** Lead with what the customer gains (ahorro, tranquilidad, control), then the
  feature.

### Headline patterns
- Benefit + reassurance: *"La energía que cuida de tu bienestar"*
- Action-oriented CTA copy: *"Contrata online", "Ahorra ahora", "Úneme gratis"*
- Question hooks: *"¿Cuánto pagaré con este plan?", "¿Por qué elegir…?"*

## Photography
- **Real people, real moments.** Natural light, candid warmth, genuine smiles — families,
  professionals, athletes. Diverse and inclusive.
- **Bright, airy, hopeful.** Greenery, homes, outdoors, renewable landscapes.
- **Editorial crops.** Full-bleed image bands with centred white overlay text (see
  `.ib-band` pattern). Keep the focal subject clear of the text.
- Avoid stiff stock, harsh studio lighting, or cold/desaturated grades.

## Illustration & icons
- Friendly, rounded, semi-3D icons in brand green with orange/blue accents (leaf, house, plug,
  solar, EV, headset). Soft shadows, approachable geometry.
- Decorative flowing line motifs (thin green curved "energy" lines) as background texture in
  calm sections.

## Color usage in layouts
- **Green** carries the brand: primary CTAs, links, stats, key accents.
- **Deep forest green** for high-impact promo bands, heroes, and the footer.
- **Orange** as an energetic secondary CTA / attention accent — use sparingly.
- **Blue** for informational accents and links on dark.
- **Pastel tints** (mint, peach, sky, sand) to segment sections and audience groups softly.
- Keep white space generous; let one dominant color lead each section.

## Accessibility of tone & contrast
- Body text meets **WCAG AA** against its background (see `docs/index.html` contrast notes).
- Never rely on color alone to convey meaning; pair with text/icon.
